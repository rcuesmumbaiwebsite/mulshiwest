    Glamping

    Mulshi West is a unique DIY Glamping site 40 km from Lonavala. With a small hamlet for company, the site has nothing but nature around it. Two luxury tents are set up on 43 acres of wilderness. The property is nestled in the densely forested Sahyadri Range on the edge of the Deccan Plateau. It is a biodiversity hotspot, and the entire property is contiguous with a forest that extends from Mulshi to the Konkan plains 700 m below. There are no waiters or cooks on site. Village meals can be arranged upon request, and we encourage you to cook for the family in our kitchen with a view! The entire site has very poor mobile network, but a very rich connection with nature. Leaving the city behind is not an option.

    Tents

    NO SHOES IN THE TENT

    Mulshi West is surrounded by trails that makes our shoes muddy. We therefore do not allow shoes in the tent. Please take off your shoes and place then in the shoe rack or outside the tent. If you prefer, you are welcome to bring a pair of clean flip flops to use inside the tent. Before wearing your shoes in the morning, please make sure that nothing has crept in there overnight for warmth.

    Food

    Food

    MEALS

    We encourage you to cook your own meals. There is a small kitchen equipped with a hot plate, cooking gas, microwave and electric kettle. There is basic cutlery, crockery, pots and pans. There is a refrigerator and water filter as well. Guests are also welcome to use complimentary tea, coffee, sugar, cooking oil, condiments, and herbs and spices from the kitchen. There is also a small barbecue. Please use it carefully and pack it away once you are done. You will need to bring your own coal. Home-cooked, wholesome, village meals are available on request. You will need to inform our staff well in advance to place an order. We find the meals tasty and wholesome however we do not provide any assurance about tastes and quantities. All meals have prefixed rates so that you don’t get taken for a ride but there is no involvement of Mulshi West beyond that.

    Do’s and Dont’s

    The property is nestled in the lap of a dense forest on the edge of the Deccan Plateau. It is a biodiversity hotspot and the entire property is contiguous with a thick forest to the south. All mammals, reptiles and insects and birds that roam the forest also roam Mulshi West. Nothing is off limits to the forest residents – not even the tents so be careful at all times.

    DIY GLAMPING,
    NOT RESORT LIFE

    The tents at Mulshi West offer visitors comfort and convenience over regular camping tents. They have an attached toilet and bathroom and basic electric fixtures. The tents and site are not a resort-like setting with waiters and staff to cater to needs of clients. The staff are only required to furnish a clean tent on arrival and to help you with getting food if you choose not to cook for yourself. Staff can also help to plan and organise treks and excursion to surrounding areas of interest. Everything else is DIY.

    ORIENTATION & INSPECTION
    BEFORE GETTING COMFY

    ORIENTATION & INSPECTION BEFORE GETTING COMFY: The tents are cleaned everyday before guests arrive. Our local staff of 3 are all from the village of Ekola and are fluent in Marathi and Hindi. English will be a struggle. The staff will explain the Dos and Donts when you arrive. They will take you to your tents and show you the beds, bathrooms and electric switches. Please inspect the bathroom and tent cleanliness and all the beds linen during orientation. If need be, please ask for it to be cleaned or replaced. Sometimes, a critter can sneak in if the door is left open. It is a good practice to have the caretaker inspect the tent thoroughly during orientation to ensure that there are no ants, mice or scorpions sharing your room.

    NO FOOD IN THE TENT.
    EVER

    It is imperative that no food be taken into the tent at any time. Not for children to nibble at nor for midnight snacks. All food is to be stored in the kitchen and to be eaten in the patio area or barbeque area. Food in the tent even if unopened is very likely to attract mice followed by snakes.

    SMOKING &
    ACLOHOL

    The tents are off-limits for alcohol consumption and a very strict no-smoking zone. Smoking is a serious fire hazard with the tent material, the bedding is also flammable and a single entry and exit to the tent put visitors at risk. We have built and nurtured the site to be happy place for families to connect with nature and with each other. We do serve alcohol or tobacco on the site. Ekola is strictly a DRUG FREE ZONE with a zero-tolerance policy. All offenders and the booking party will be removed reported to the local gram panchayat and Paud Police Station.
